protein

Protein is an absolute essential in bodybuilding as this is the key to gains! A constant supply of Protein throughout the day is important for optimum muscle growth. Protein is composed by amino acids which is the foundation of your muscles and body. Without them, it would be impossible to build, repair or even maintain muscle tissue.

Protein is also made up of Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen and Nitrogen. Having a sufficient amount of protein will put you in a anabolic state that'll allow you to build muscle. On the other hand, if you don't consume an ample amount of protein this could result in you being in a catabolic state. This causes your body to break down your muscles for a source of energy.

carbohydrates

Carbohydrates play an important role for bodybuilders. They give the body energy to deal with the rigors of training and recovery. carbohydrates also promote insulin, a hormone enabling cells to get the glucose they need. Insulin also carries amino acids into cells and promotes protein synthesis which is imperative if your goal is to increase muscle mass.

fat

Packing nine calories per gram, fat is a more concentrated source of energy than both protein and carbohydrates. In order to gain mass, as many bodybuilders are doing, you need to be in a caloric surplus. In other words, you need to be consuming more calories than you're burning and the additional calories will be used to, in this case, store fat. In other cases, the extra energy can be used to build muscle.

Why do I need water?

Water consumption is absolutely essential and is as equally important as the nutrients above, but one of the most overlooked. One of the greatest portion of our body weight is made up of water, at around 65-70%. Water flushes metabolic waste products and regulates all body functions as well as help your kidneys work their magic.

Most importantly, major decreases in performance can occur due to even slight dehydration, impacting your athletic potential. If your body isn't getting the fluids it needs, you won't be able to push hard in the gym and achieve your goal.

What's lean body mass?

Your lean body mass is the amount of weight your body holds that isn't fat. The goal of any bodybuilder or fitness fanatic is to lose weight whilst maintaining their lean body mass, so essentially it's just decreasing your body fat percentage.
